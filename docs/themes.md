# Map Themes Guide

How India Lives organises 205 maps into 12 thematic sections. Here's what each theme covers and why it matters.

## 1. Demography (13 maps)
**Population, urbanisation, migration, linguistic diversity — the fundamental shape of who lives where.**

Key indicators: Median age, sex ratio, child sex ratio, transgender population, decadal growth rate, urbanisation, migration, linguistic diversity.

## 2. Food & Culture (15 maps)
**What India eats, drinks, and avoids — vegetarianism, alcohol, tobacco, and dietary diversity by state.**

Key indicators: Vegetarianism rates, alcohol consumption, tobacco use, newspaper readership, dietary diversity, food habits.

## 3. Economy (42 maps)
**Income, employment, informality, infrastructure — the vast economic distance between states.**

The largest theme, reflecting how economic outcomes vary most dramatically across India. Key indicators: Poverty (MPI), informal workforce, income tax filers, MGNREGA days, digital access, housing quality, consumption expenditure, per-capita income.

## 4. Health (30 maps)
**Mortality, nutrition, disease burden, healthcare access — where you are born still determines how long you live.**

Key indicators: Life expectancy, infant mortality, stunting, wasting, anaemia, out-of-pocket health spending, doctor density, mental health resources.

## 5. Gender (23 maps)
**Women's work, safety, autonomy, and the sharp variation in what it means to be a woman across Indian states.**

Key indicators: Female labour force participation, domestic violence, women's decision-making autonomy, property ownership, media exposure gap, child marriage.

## 6. Caste (10 maps)
**SC/ST demographics, discrimination, violence, and the persistence of caste in shaping life outcomes.**

Key indicators: SC/ST population share, atrocity rates, conviction rates, educational attainment gaps, poverty overlap.

## 7. Religion (10 maps)
**Religious composition, diversity, and the geography of faith across India's states.**

Key indicators: Religious composition by state, religious diversity index, interfaith demographics.

## 8. Education (13 maps)
**Literacy, enrolment, dropout rates, digital access — the building blocks of opportunity.**

Key indicators: Literacy rates, dropout rates, pupil-teacher ratio, school infrastructure, higher education enrolment, UDISE+ indicators.

## 9. Environment (15 maps)
**Forest cover, air quality, groundwater, climate vulnerability — the environmental context of development.**

Key indicators: Forest cover, PM2.5 exposure, groundwater depletion, night light intensity, climate vulnerability, disaster exposure.

## 10. Democracy (13 maps)
**Voter turnout, political representation, and the health of democratic participation across states.**

Key indicators: Voter turnout, women MLAs, criminal cases against legislators, fact-checking coverage, RTI usage.

## 11. Social Protection (10 maps)
**Government schemes, safety nets, and how effectively the state reaches its most vulnerable citizens.**

Key indicators: MGNREGA performance, Jal Jeevan Mission coverage, PMFBY claims, ration card coverage, pension access.

## 12. Reproductive Choices (11 maps)
**Contraception, sterilisation, family planning — where reproductive autonomy varies most sharply.**

Key indicators: Modern contraceptive use, female sterilisation rates, unmet need for family planning, teenage pregnancy, HIV prevalence.
